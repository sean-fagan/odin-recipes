Chicken Marsala

Chicken Marsala is a classic Italian-American dish that showcases tender, pan-seared chicken breasts simmered in a rich and savory Marsala wine sauce. The chicken is typically seasoned and lightly floured before being cooked to a golden brown, creating a delicious crust. The sauce, made with sweet Marsala wine, mushrooms, garlic, and often a hint of cream, complements the chicken beautifully, imparting deep, earthy flavors.

The dish is usually garnished with fresh parsley, adding a pop of color and a touch of brightness. Served over a bed of pasta or alongside creamy mashed potatoes, Chicken Marsala is a comforting and elegant meal that perfectly balances savory and slightly sweet notes, making it a favorite in many homes and restaurants alike.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 4 medium skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - pounded 1/4 inch thick
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup Marsala wine
  • 1/4 cup cooking sherry

Steps

  1. Mix together all your seasonings with the flour
  2. Coat the chicken in the flour
  3. Melt butter and olive oil on skillet, then place in your chicken till it gets light brown
  4. Flip the chicken and add your mushrooms. Then add in your wine and sherry
  5. Cover the pan and let the chicken simmer for about 10 minutes until cooked fully
  6. Enjoy!
